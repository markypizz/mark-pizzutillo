Bio

Hello! I'm Mark. I'm a 22 year old software engineer currently residing in Austin, TX. Currently I work for IBM, writing code for DRAM initialization on IBM Power & Z Systems.

I grew up in southern New Jersey, and graduated from The Pennsylvania State University in 3.5 years with a B.S. in Computer Engineering, and a minor in Music Performance. In my time at Penn State, I worked as a undergraduate research assistant in the Penn State Mobile Computing and Networking laboratory, where I found a side-passion for writing mobile apps (more on that later!). During some phenomenal courses, I was fortunate to work on a variety of projects ranging from an FPGA CPU, to operating system components in C and x86, to iOS Applications in Swift, to an Arduino-powered water-piping system, and so much more.

Outside of work & school, I have an obsession with learning new areas of computer science and engineering. I have written 2 sizeable iOS applications, a 3D Chess game and a musician's toolkit including a metronome, tuner, and audio recorder. On the other side of that coin, I've spent some time learning Ionic+Angular, with plans to hopefully write a cross-platform app of some kind, either with that, or with React Native, Flutter, or wherever the wind takes me. I've spent some time trying to learn languages I don't work with normally, such as Swift (which I love), Go, HTML/CSS/JS. However, my bread & butter will always be C/C++, and Java.

One of my more recent endeavors is to revisit my old FPGA CPU project, but re-do it. I've begun writing my own basic ISA, implement this in a SystemVerilog CPU, and will have it read/write data to/from an NVRAM dip. All of this will just be implemented on breadboards for the time being. Some long-term plans may include keyboard support and some kind of shell to write basic programs.

Rants aside, hope you enjoy my latest endeavor, creating a website :-)
Not everything here is from scratch, but it's a good introduction for me into HTML/CSS.
